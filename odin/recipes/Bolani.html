Bolani

Bolani is a traditional Afghani dish that consists of thin,pan-fried turnovers filled with various ingredients, such as potatoes, leeks, or spinach. The crispy exterior encases a flavorful , spiced filling , making it a popular appetizer or snack in Afghan cuisine. In this section we will see how to make Potatoe Bolani.

    Ingredients:

  • Dough
    1. 2 cups all-purpose flour
    2. 1 teaspoon salt
    3. water
  • For the filling:
    1. 3 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
    2. 1 onion, finely chopped
    3. 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
    4. 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    5. 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    6. Salt to taste
    7. Vegetable oil for frying

    How to make it ?

  1. Make the dough:
    - In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. - Gradually add water while kneading the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover the dough and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling:
    - In a separate bowl, mix the mashed potatoes, chopped onion, minced garlic, ground coriander, ground cumin, and salt. Ensure the filling is thoroughly combined and seasoned to taste.
  3. Assemble the bolani:
    - Divide the dough into several small balls. On a floured surface, roll out each ball into a thin, circular disc.
  4. Add the filling:
    - Place a portion of the prepared filling onto one half of the rolled-out dough, leaving space around the edges.
  5. Seal the bolani:
    - Fold the other half of the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape. Press the edges firmly to seal them
  6. Fry the bolani:
    - In a large skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the bolani and fry until golden brown on both sides.
  7. Serve:
    - Remove the bolani from the skillet and place it on paper towels to drain any excess oil. Cut the bolani into wedges and serve with a side of plain yogurt or a dipping sauce of your choice.
