Kabab


Kebabs are a staple in Afghan cuisine, featuring tender, marinated meat skewered and grilled to perfection. The aromatic spices and flavorful marinade infuse the meat, creating a delightful medley of tastes and textures.

    Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs lamb, cut into cubes
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers, soaked in water

How to make it ?

  1. Prepare the marinade:
    - In a bowl, combine the chopped onion, minced garlic, plain yogurt, olive oil, ground coriander, ground cumin, ground turmeric, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix the ingredients thoroughly to form a smooth marinade.
  2. Marinate the lamb:
    - Place the lamb cubes in the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover the bowl and marinate the meat for at least 2-4 hours, or preferably overnight, in the refrigerator to allow the flavors to permeate the meat.
  3. Skewer the lamb:
    - Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated lamb cubes onto the soaked skewers, ensuring an even distribution of meat on each skewer.
  4. Grill the kebabs:
    - Place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook the kebabs for approximately 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the meat is charred on the outside and cooked to the desired level of doneness.
  5. Serve:
    - Once grilled to perfection, remove the kebabs from the heat and allow them to rest for a few minutes. Serve the succulent lamb kebabs with a side of naan bread, rice, or a fresh salad for a truly delightful and satisfying meal.
